# Acme Corp Employee Handbook

Welcome to Acme Corp! This handbook outlines the policies, procedures, and benefits that apply to all employees.

## Code of Conduct

All employees are expected to act with integrity, respect, and professionalism at all times. Harassment, discrimination, or retaliation of any kind will not be tolerated. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### Dress Code

Acme Corp maintains a business-casual dress code Monday through Thursday. Fridays are designated as casual dress days. Employees in client-facing roles should dress appropriately for meetings. Safety gear (hard hats, steel-toed boots) is mandatory in warehouse and factory areas.

### Workplace Safety

All employees must complete safety orientation within their first week. Report any unsafe conditions to your manager or the Safety Committee immediately. Emergency exits must remain clear at all times. Fire drills are conducted quarterly—participation is mandatory.

## Compensation and Benefits

### Salary Structure

Salaries are reviewed annually during the Q1 performance review cycle. Pay grades range from Level 1 (entry) to Level 8 (executive). Cost-of-living adjustments are applied each January based on CPI data. Overtime is calculated at 1.5x the hourly rate for non-exempt employees working beyond 40 hours per week.

### Health Insurance

Acme Corp provides comprehensive health insurance through BlueCross BlueShield. Coverage begins on the first day of the month following your start date. Plans include HMO, PPO, and High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) options. The company covers 80% of individual premiums and 60% of dependent premiums. Open enrollment occurs annually in November.

### Retirement Plans

Employees are eligible to participate in the company's 401(k) plan after 90 days of employment. Acme Corp matches employee contributions up to 4% of gross salary. Vesting follows a 3-year graded schedule: 33% after year 1, 66% after year 2, and 100% after year 3. Employees may also contribute to a Roth 401(k) option.

### Paid Time Off

Full-time employees receive the following PTO allocations per year:
- **0-2 years tenure**: 15 days
- **3-5 years tenure**: 20 days
- **6+ years tenure**: 25 days

PTO must be requested at least two weeks in advance for absences of 3 or more consecutive days. Unused PTO may be rolled over up to a maximum of 5 days. PTO is paid out upon separation from the company.

## Remote Work Policy

### Eligibility

Remote work is available for roles that do not require on-site presence. Employees must have completed at least 6 months of tenure and received a "Meets Expectations" or higher rating in their most recent performance review. Manager approval is required, and remote work arrangements are reviewed quarterly.

### Equipment and Expenses

Acme Corp provides a one-time $500 home office stipend for remote employees. The company will ship a laptop, monitor, and peripherals. Employees are responsible for maintaining a reliable internet connection (minimum 50 Mbps download). Monthly internet reimbursement of up to $75 is available upon submission of receipts.

### Communication Expectations

Remote employees must be available on Slack during core hours (10 AM - 4 PM local time). Video must be enabled during all team meetings. Response time for Slack messages should not exceed 30 minutes during core hours. All remote employees are required to attend in-person team offsites held twice per year.

## Leave Policies

### Parental Leave

Acme Corp offers 16 weeks of paid parental leave for the birth or adoption of a child. Both primary and secondary caregivers are eligible. Leave may be taken consecutively or intermittently within the first 12 months. Employees must provide at least 30 days notice when possible.

### Sick Leave

Employees receive 10 paid sick days per year. A doctor's note is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days. Sick leave may be used for personal illness, medical appointments, or caring for an immediate family member. Unused sick days do not roll over.

### Bereavement Leave

Employees are granted up to 5 paid days for the loss of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling). Up to 3 paid days are granted for the loss of an extended family member (grandparent, in-law, aunt, uncle). Additional unpaid leave may be requested through HR.

## Performance Management

### Review Cycle

Performance reviews are conducted twice per year: a formal annual review in Q1 and a mid-year check-in during Q3. Reviews are based on goal completion, core competencies, and peer feedback. Self-assessments are due two weeks before the review meeting.

### Promotion Criteria

Promotions are based on consistent performance at the next level for at least 6 months. A sponsoring manager must submit a promotion packet including evidence of impact, peer endorsements, and a development plan. Promotion committees meet quarterly to review submissions.

### Performance Improvement Plans

Employees who receive a "Below Expectations" rating will be placed on a 60-day Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). PIPs include specific, measurable goals and bi-weekly check-ins with the manager. Successful completion results in removal from the PIP; failure may result in termination.

## IT and Security Policies

### Acceptable Use

Company devices and networks are for business use only. Personal use should be minimal and must not interfere with work. Downloading unauthorized software is prohibited. All company data must be stored on approved cloud platforms (Google Workspace, AWS S3).

### Password Policy

Passwords must be at least 14 characters and include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Passwords must be changed every 90 days. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all company systems. Password managers (1Password) are provided free of charge.

### Data Classification

Data is classified into four tiers:
- **Public**: Marketing materials, blog posts
- **Internal**: Company-wide memos, org charts
- **Confidential**: Financial reports, employee records
- **Restricted**: Trade secrets, encryption keys, customer PII

Handling procedures for each tier are documented in the Data Governance Wiki. Unauthorized sharing of Confidential or Restricted data is a terminable offense.

### Incident Response

All security incidents must be reported to the Security Operations Center (SOC) within 1 hour of discovery. The SOC will triage incidents using a P1-P4 severity scale. P1 incidents (data breach, ransomware) trigger an immediate all-hands war room. Post-incident reviews are conducted within 48 hours, and findings are shared company-wide.

## Expense Policy

### Travel

All business travel must be pre-approved by your manager. Book flights and hotels through the company's Concur portal. Economy class is standard for flights under 6 hours; business class may be approved for longer flights. Per diem rates follow GSA guidelines and vary by city.

### Meals and Entertainment

Meal expenses are reimbursable up to $50 per person per meal during business travel. Client entertainment requires prior VP-level approval for expenses exceeding $200. Alcohol is limited to 2 drinks per person during client dinners. All receipts must be submitted within 30 days via Concur.

### Reimbursement Process

Submit expense reports within 30 days of incurring the expense. Attach original receipts for any expense over $25. Reports are reviewed by your manager and Finance within 5 business days. Approved reimbursements are deposited in the next payroll cycle.

## Learning and Development

### Training Budget

Each employee receives an annual training budget of $2,000. This can be used for courses, conferences, books, or certification exams. Pre-approval from your manager is required for expenses over $500. Unused budget does not roll over to the next year.

### Mentorship Program

Acme Corp runs a formal mentorship program with 6-month cohorts starting in January and July. Mentees are paired with senior leaders outside their direct reporting line. Each pair is expected to meet at least twice per month. Program applications are reviewed by the People Team.

### Internal Mobility

Employees who have been in their current role for at least 12 months may apply for internal transfers. Internal candidates receive priority consideration for open positions. A 2-week transition period is standard; longer transitions require VP approval. Compensation is adjusted to match the new role's pay band.
